The Technology We Have Now

What do we use now to help sick animals?

Apps and Websites

Educational

The Merk Veterinary Manual can be very useful for upcoming veterinarians as it contains a diverse list of medical ailments for a variety of animals. The website offers informative videos, quizzes, and a list of several different veterinary topics which users have free access to. This free and informative website would be perfect for veterinary students or those who just want to learn more about animal ailments.

The Pet First Aid is a free app that provides quick and easy guides for emergency situations. Owners will be given guides on how monitor and detect vital signs as well as how to provide first aid for their cats and dogs. Additionally to the app there is also a free at & Dog Aid Online Training that teaches the basics of caring for pets.

Assisting

Otto is a large and comprehensive platform that is more targeted for practicing vets and their businesses. Otto helps communication across veterinary clinics and patients to improve the care of pets. Appointments, meetings, calls, and more can be easily arranged.

BT-LINK MOBILE is an app that allows users to monitor any of Bionet's patient monitors through network connection. The connected smartphone or tablet will then be able to receive alarms from the monitor even when in different locations. The app then captures collected data as images for later viewing.

Precise Imaging

Imaging procedures are a variety of different tests that form an image of areas inside the body using high energy waves. You might be familiar with the term "x-ray" or "MRI" as they are often used in the medical field. Actually, x-rays the most commonly used precise imaging procedure used in veterinary. These procedures are extremely helpful for veternarians to look take a look inside the animal's body without any invasive procedures. They can alow doctors to look for any discrepancies within the patient as well as injuries.

3D Printing

3D printing first appeared in the medical world in the 1990s and has also been appearing in the veterinary world since 2018. 3D printing can create demonstrative models of skulls and bones for educational purposes and for surgeons to analyze what is wrong and prepare for the actual operation. Custom transplants and surgical parts have been made as well.

Robots

Robot-assisted sugeries are becoming more common in the medical world. These robots allow for smaller and more precise operations instead of having large operation. Robots can also help with operations that require small and precise movement. Cameras on the robot allow for better viewing inside smaller spaces, and the use of cameras means that smaller incisions are made, decreasing the chance of infection.
